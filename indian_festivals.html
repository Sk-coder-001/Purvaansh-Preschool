Diwali

Diwali, also known as Deepavali, is a major Hindu festival celebrated with great enthusiasm across India. It marks the victory of light over darkness and good over evil, symbolized by lighting lamps, bursting fireworks, and sharing sweets. For more details, visit the
Wikipedia page.

Holi

Holi, the festival of colors, is celebrated by throwing colored powders and water at each other. It signifies the arrival of spring and the triumph of good over evil. The joyous festival is marked by music, dance, and vibrant colors. Learn more on the
Wikipedia page.

Eid al-Fitr

Eid al-Fitr is a significant Islamic festival that marks the end of Ramadan, the holy month of fasting. It is a time for communal prayers, feasting, and giving to charity. Families and friends come together to celebrate this joyous occasion. For more information, visit the
Wikipedia page.

Navratri

Navratri is a Hindu festival dedicated to the worship of the goddess Durga. It spans nine nights and includes fasting, dancing, and religious rituals. Each night represents a different form of the goddess and is celebrated with specific customs. Discover more on the
Wikipedia page.

Pongal

Pongal is a four-day harvest festival celebrated in Tamil Nadu. It includes cooking a special dish called Pongal, which is made from newly harvested rice. The festival is a time to express gratitude for the bounty of the harvest and to celebrate the agricultural traditions. Read more on the
Wikipedia page.

Ganesh Chaturthi

Ganesh Chaturthi is a Hindu festival celebrating the birth of Lord Ganesha. It involves creating and worshipping clay idols of Ganesha, followed by immersion in water at the end of the festival. The festival is marked by vibrant processions and public celebrations. For details, visit the
Wikipedia page.

Raksha Bandhan

Raksha Bandhan is a festival celebrating the bond between brothers and sisters. Sisters tie a protective thread (rakhi) around their brothers' wrists, and brothers give gifts in return. The festival emphasizes familial love and protection. Learn more on the
Wikipedia page.

Janmashtami

Janmashtami celebrates the birth of Lord Krishna. The festival involves fasting, singing devotional songs, and reenacting episodes from Krishna's life. It is particularly famous for the "Dahi Handi" event where teams form human pyramids to break a pot of curd. For more, check the
Wikipedia page.

Onam

Onam is the harvest festival celebrated in Kerala, known for its vibrant celebrations including the Onam Sadya (feast), floral decorations, and traditional boat races. It commemorates the homecoming of the mythical King Mahabali. Explore more on the
Wikipedia page.

Baisakhi

Baisakhi is a harvest festival celebrated in Punjab, marking the beginning of the harvest season and the Sikh New Year. It is also significant for commemorating the formation of the Khalsa in 1699. The festival includes traditional dances, fairs, and religious ceremonies. Learn more on the
Wikipedia page.

Christmas

Christmas is celebrated by Christians across India to commemorate the birth of Jesus Christ. The festival includes attending church services, decorating Christmas trees, and exchanging gifts. It is a time of joy and communal celebration. For more information, visit the
Wikipedia page.
